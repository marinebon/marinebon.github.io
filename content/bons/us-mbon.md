## \*\*US\*\* MBON

The \*\*U.S. MBON\*\* projects are integrating independent historical and
current biology and ecosystem surveys with new observations, and
expanding application of remote sensing methods, novel molecular (eDNA)
technologies, traditional environmental research tools, and coordinated
experiments.

#### Benefits of \*\*MBON:\*\*

MBON projects, partners and data play an important role in enabling the
scientific community to describe the relationships between biodiversity,
organism abundance, system productivity, ecosystem health and ecosystem
services.
Visit the [Join MBON page](index.html%3Fp=8427.html) for more
information about the benefits of MBON.

#### Sponsors:

U.S. MBON projects have been funded since 2013 under the National
Oceanographic Partnership Program (FY14 RFP NOAA-NOS-IOOS-2014-2003803
and FY19 ONR Announcement \# N00014-18-S-B007).
U.S. MBON represents a partnership among NOAA, NASA, BOEM, and ONR, with
coordination provided by the U.S. Integrated Ocean Observing System
(U.S. IOOS) Program Office.
